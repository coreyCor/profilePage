How did you become a web developer?

I've always had a great interest in computers and software technologies. I remember playing The Oregon Trail in grade school and one day asking a teacher, "How does the game know what to do when I answer the questions?" Mrs. Beatty replied "Computers are smart but they have to be told what to do and how to perform even the most simple of tasks." That was also around the start of the World Wide Web! Or at least when it became easier for the average family to navigate. Around 1995 we got our first computer. Right away I was hooked. In High School after learning how to navigate notePad on Windows, I started to create very plain but fun to show off HTML pages. After highschool and pursuing a degree in Veterinary Medicine, I worked for about 3 local Veterinary hospitals over the course of about ten years. Always the autodidact I still strive learn and expand my views of the world through education. While still working in the Veterinary field I casually studied and became more interested in programming and web development. One day I just decided that I have so many ideas for websites and apps that the only way I am going to achieve the level of professionalism needed to design these ideas is to attend a school/bootcamp to become a full stack web developer. After a formal education, I now get to do what I love full time and help contribute to the online world as a professionally trained full stack web developer.

What is your design process?

My design process is simple, I meet with the client when it's convenient for them to find out what their needs are. From there we define what they need their website or application to do. We'll specify the actions and theme as well as the content. I design all my web sites and applications with the latest programming technologies allowing them to be accessible and predictable from any device.

Bio

Born and raised in Southern California, I always had a great respect for nature and the local wildlife. So it was only natural that when I was old enough I would apply at the local Veterinary Hospital. I was trained by a wonderful staff in almost every aspect of veterinary medicine and hospital management. After High School I pursued a degree in Psychology and Biology and was happy and interested, but post school my work life was unfulfilling. In Medicine there is not much opportunity to express yourself and most of the time it is frowned upon. Web development offers a technical aspect with protocols and strict rules, but it also gives you the chance to express yourself. I am a very hard worker and love to teach myself new things so I feel that web development is the perfect career choice for me as it is continuously changing and being updated.
